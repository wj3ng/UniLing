Contests

Linguistics contests are a good way to prove your puzzle solving ability under a timed environment. The main linguistics contest for American high school (and middle school) students is the North American Computational Linguistics Olympiad (NACLO for short). The top finalists of NACLO qualify for the International Linguistics Olympiad (IOL), the most prestigious linguistics competition for high schoolers (and middle schoolers).

North American Computational Linguistics Olympiad

NACLO was first organized in 2007 to select US and Canadian students to go to the IOL. There are many contest sites around the continent. The nearest site from Uni High is UCI. The current format hosts an Open Round in January, which any student can participate in for free. The highest scorers in the continent qualify for the Invitational Round in March. The top eight US students and top four Canadian students get to represent their countries at the IOL, which takes place in a different host country every summer.
